Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023

Comprehensive summary of India's data protection law

Status: Presidential assent received August 11, 2023. Commencement date to be notified.

Executive Overview

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 establishes a comprehensive framework for protecting personal data in the digital ecosystem. It balances individual rights with legitimate data processing needs, introduces the Data Protection Board of India, and prescribes significant penalties for non-compliance.

10 Core Principles

1. Lawfulness: Process only with consent or legitimate use
2. Purpose Limitation: Use data only for stated purpose
3. Data Minimization: Collect only necessary data
4. Accuracy: Ensure data is complete and accurate
5. Storage Limitation: Retain only as long as needed
6. Security: Implement safeguards against breaches
7. Accountability: Data Fiduciary responsible for compliance
8. Transparency: Clear notice to Data Principals
9. Individual Rights: Access, correction, erasure, grievance
10. Special Protection: Enhanced safeguards for children

RBI Digital Lending Guidelines 2025

Directions on Digital Lending and related matters

Applicability: All Regulated Entities (Commercial Banks, Co-operative Banks, NBFCs, All-India FIs)

Overview

These directions address concerns in digital lending including unbridled activities by third parties, mis-selling, data privacy breaches, unfair business conduct, and charging of excessive interest rates. They establish standards for RE-LSP arrangements, borrower protection, and data handling.
